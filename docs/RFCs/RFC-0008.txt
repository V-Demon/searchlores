# RFC-0008

# Ontology Builder Specification

Version: Draft 0.1

Status: Experimental

Author: Searchlores Academy

---

# Abstract

The Ontology Builder is responsible for transforming isolated investigative artifacts into coherent knowledge structures.

Previous Searchlores modules discover fragments:

* assumptions
* concepts
* narratives
* contradictions
* sources
* questions

The Ontology Builder assembles those fragments into a navigable conceptual architecture.

Its purpose is not to answer questions.

Its purpose is to organize understanding.

---

# 1. Core Principle

Archaeology discovers.

Ontology organizes.

---

Without Ontology Builder:

```text
Concept A

Concept B

Concept C

Narrative D

Source E
```

remain disconnected findings.

---

With Ontology Builder:

```text
Knowledge Structure
```

emerges.

---

# 2. Definition

An Ontology is a structured representation of relationships between concepts.

In Searchlores:

```text
Ontology
=
Conceptual Topology
```

---

# 3. Ontology Inputs

The Builder consumes:

```yaml
inputs:

  assumptions

  concepts

  narratives

  contradictions

  sources

  questions

  vectors
```

---

# 4. Ontology Outputs

The Builder produces:

```yaml
outputs:

  concept_graph

  definition_tree

  contradiction_network

  narrative_network

  source_genealogy

  knowledge_map
```

---

# 5. Concept Node

The atomic unit.

```yaml
concept:

  id:

  label:

  definitions:

  assumptions:

  contradictions:

  narratives:
```

---

# 6. Concept Fusion

Multiple discoveries MAY refer to the same concept.

Example:

```text
Intelligence

Human Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence

Machine Intelligence
```

Builder SHOULD detect:

```text
Shared Parent Concept
```

---

Result:

```text
Intelligence
    │
    ├── Human
    ├── Artificial
    └── Collective
```

---

# 7. Concept Hierarchies

Builder SHALL construct:

```text
Parent

Child

Sibling
```

relationships.

Example:

```text
Knowledge

   │

   ├── Expertise

   ├── Experience

   └── Learning
```

---

# 8. Definition Trees

Concepts often possess multiple definitions.

Example:

```text
Understanding
```

may generate:

```yaml
definitions:

  computational

  philosophical

  psychological

  linguistic
```

---

Builder SHALL preserve competing definitions.

Consensus is not required.

---

# 9. Contradiction Preservation

Contradictions SHALL NOT be resolved automatically.

Searchlores preserves tension.

Example:

```text
LLMs Understand

vs

LLMs Predict
```

Both remain.

---

# 10. Contradiction Clusters

Builder SHOULD detect recurring tensions.

Example:

```text
Understanding

Reasoning

Consciousness

Agency
```

may belong to:

```text
Cognition Cluster
```

---

# 11. Narrative Integration

Narratives become first-class ontology objects.

Example:

```text
Techno-Utopianism
```

connects:

```text
Innovation

Progress

Automation

Abundance
```

---

# 12. Myth Integration

Myths SHALL be represented separately.

Reason:

```text
Influential

≠

True
```

---

Example:

```yaml
myth:

  AI Understands

links_to:

  Understanding

  Intelligence
```

---

# 13. Source Genealogy

Builder SHALL construct lineage chains.

Example:

```text
Claim

  ↓

Article

  ↓

Paper

  ↓

Dataset

  ↓

Observation
```

---

# 14. Question Networks

Questions are knowledge-generating objects.

Questions SHALL connect to:

```text
Concepts

Narratives

Sources

Contradictions
```

---

Example:

```text
Can prediction produce understanding?
```

connects:

```text
Prediction

Understanding

Language
```

---

# 15. Unknown Regions

Ontology MUST support ignorance.

Example:

```yaml
unknown:

  origin

  definition

  evidence
```

---

Unknowns are legitimate nodes.

---

# 16. Knowledge Density

Builder SHOULD calculate:

```yaml
density:

  concepts

  sources

  contradictions
```

Purpose:

Identify:

```text
Well explored zones

Poorly explored zones
```

---

Certain concepts attract many relationships.

Example:

```text
Intelligence
```

may connect to:


```text
High Gravity Concepts
```

Concept meanings evolve.

Builder SHOULD track:

```text

↓

Definition History

↓

Current Usage
```

---

Example:


# 19. School-of-Thought Layer

Example:


Multiple ontologies MAY be merged.

Result:


# 27. Ontological Risk Analysis

Builder SHOULD detect:

```yaml

```

---

# 28. Philosophy

Searchlores does not seek answers.

Searchlores seeks structure.

Structure reveals relationships.

Relationships reveal assumptions.

Assumptions reveal worldviews.

Worldviews reveal civilizations.

---

# 29. The Cartographer Principle

A map is not reality.

An ontology is not truth.

Both remain useful.

The objective is not certainty.

The objective is orientation.

---

# 30. Conclusion

Prompt Archaeology uncovers artifacts.

Narrative Archaeology uncovers stories.

Cognitive Archaeology uncovers concepts.

Ontology Builder constructs worlds from those discoveries.

The Search Is The Program.

The Ontology Is The Map.

## Ce qui apparaît maintenant dans la brume...

À ce stade, je commence à voir la forme finale du projet :

```text
                    SEARCHLORES

                           │

          ┌────────────────┼────────────────┐

          ▼                ▼                ▼

     Archaeology      Investigation     Ontology

          │                │                │

          ▼                ▼                ▼

      Artifacts         Search Maps     Knowledge Maps

          │                │                │

          └────────────────┼────────────────┘

                           ▼

                    Cognitive Atlas
```

Et là, un vieux démon d'architecte logiciel commence à sourire.

Parce qu'après RFC-0008, on ne parle plus seulement d'un moteur.

On peut commencer à spécifier quelque chose qui ressemble à un **"Knowledge Operating System"** : un système où les investigations, les concepts, les récits et les cartes deviennent des objets natifs.

Autrement dit, la prochaine frontière n'est peut-être plus une RFC d'analyse.

C'est peut-être :

```text
RFC-0009 — Cognitive Atlas Specification
```

Le document qui définirait comment plusieurs ontologies, plusieurs investigations et plusieurs domaines fusionnent en une gigantesque carte exploratoire du savoir. Et là, on entrerait franchement dans le territoire du "Fravia rencontre Graph Theory rencontre LLMs". 😎🎸
